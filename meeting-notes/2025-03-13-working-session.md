# Working Session — Mar 13, 2025

**Participants:** Carlos Gonzalez de Villaumbrosia, Daria, Dejan

## Key Decisions

### 1. Own product — confirmed (hybrid approach)
- **During class:** Use case studies so people see real examples (pricing, moats, trade-offs)
- **Applied work:** Participants bring their own product from their company
- **NOT entrepreneurship.** "Pick your company, pick a product, build the AI strategy around it."
- Backup examples for anyone not currently at a company — but they assume the PM role for a real product

### 2. The artifact is NOT a document
- Not a brief, not a PRD, not a dissertation
- Carlos: "I don't want this to be a document... it should feel like it has a life of its own, co-creating with you"
- A **living strategy** / **working strategy** — interactive, iterating at high frequency
- Like an "active chief of strategy" — not reviewed every 12 months
- Must include something tangible beyond text — possibly a prototype or interactive component

### 3. "Layers" → "components" or "pillars"
- Not sequential steps — interconnected pieces
- Students need to orient themselves: "I'm in this component of the strategy"
- Carlos prefers **5 components** (M1–M5 build the artifact, M6 presents it)
- Iteration order may differ from build order — should feel interconnected, not strictly linear

### 4. M5 needs more substance
- Carlos: "I want it to be something strong... connecting the context, building the systems, integrating the feedback loop"
- NOT about creating data policies or governance documents
- Instead: **building compounding systems** — systems that learn after every iteration
- The recursive system concept: how to build infrastructure that improves its own output quality as more people use it
- Connecting context across domains/teams so knowledge compounds instead of siloing
- Dejan's Spotify example resonated: 1,000 unsanctioned tools → audited down to 15, massive cost savings

### 5. Wow moments must produce something tangible
- Not just a feeling — something they take home
- Carlos: "Something tangible that they can take home as a reminder of what just happened"
- Each session's build connects to the final artifact — no starting from scratch in M6
- Students should feel progress: "This is something I didn't have yesterday"

### 6. M4 + M5 need more clarity for Carlos
- Carlos self-identified as not an expert on evals/governance yet
- Wants to see: how much of M4 comes from the existing evals program vs. what else exists
- M5: must be tangible, not theoretical. "I don't want to teach people how to create a better data policy"

### 7. Design and process approved
- Carlos loves the HTML slide design from Cursor
- "If you put this type of design with this type of instructor, with this type of advanced content — it's a magic cocktail"
- Wants Product School to adopt this repo-based approach for all courses
- Daria's new focus: build the standardized design system so enterprise team can scale it
