# AI Product Strategy — Working Session

**Date:** March 10, 2025
**Participants:** Carlos Gonzalez de Villaumbrosia, Dejan, Daria
**Context:** Kick-off alignment for the AI Product Strategy certification course

---

## Three Key Decisions

### 1. External focus, not internal

Carlos's directive: the course should focus on building AI-native products (external product strategy), not internal team transformation. Internal topics are already covered by AIPC and other Product School programs.

> "I would maybe focus this program more on external elements. If we put the focus on the outside — you are building AI-native products — that is going to be a much more powerful proposition and unique compared to the other programs that already cover some of the internal stuff, like AIPC."
> — Carlos

**Impact:** All module framing, learning objectives, and exercises should be audited for internal vs. external orientation. The "What is the goal?" paragraph in the syllabus needs a rewrite.

### 2. Dejan leads content, Daria supports

Original model (Daria leads, Dejan advises) was reversed. Carlos recognized that product judgment — what's advanced, what's right, what's differentiated — requires Dejan's expertise. Same model as the Vibe Coding course.

> "That judgment piece we always talk about... is yours, Dejan. So you need to define the path, give us that judgment, and maybe set the conditions so she can accelerate certain parts for you. But you have to come first."
> — Carlos

**Working model:**
- Dejan owns content direction and judgment calls
- Daria supports, accelerates, and executes
- GitHub repo is single source of truth (same pattern as Vibe Coding)
- Tuesday/Friday sessions for review with Carlos

### 3. Target audience is senior + AI-aware

Participants have completed AIPC or equivalent. They understand AI fundamentals. This is an advanced certification.

> "I would assume that people who are joining this program are not only senior product managers, but they are aware of AI, they know the basics, and if not, they should go back to take that AIPC."
> — Carlos

**Impact:** No need to teach foundational AI concepts. Can assume fluency with AI tooling. Lean harder into the "this isn't a beginner course" positioning.

## Carlos's Original Module Vision

For reference — Carlos's thinking before the session, based on research of Substack (Lenny, Akash, Peter Jang Howell), Maven top sellers, and Reforge patterns:

1. **Discovery** — Value is added upstream; demonstrate how to apply AI-powered discovery to strategy
2. **AI Moats** — Where you have the right to win; switching costs, proprietary data, flywheels
3. **Unit Economics** — AI pricing for external products + internal cost thinking
4. **Evals** — Connects to Product School's dedicated evals course
5. **Agentic Workflows & Governance** — Practical orchestration, not theory
6. **Roadmap** — How to feed everything into a roadmap

Carlos confirmed Dejan's arc (Mindset → Strategy → Money → Trust → Risk → Leadership) aligns well with this vision.
